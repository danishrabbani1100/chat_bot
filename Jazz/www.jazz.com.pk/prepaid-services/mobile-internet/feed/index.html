Introduction to 3G

What is 3G?

3G is state of the art mobile technology which gives you an enhanced internet experience on your mobile device. The most significant benefit is high speed access to video content along with generally faster mobile internet access. Some of services and applications which could be benefited from 3G are:

  • Full song downloads – a 3MB song which used to take 1min, will now take about 6 sec
  • Mobile TV – you can watch TV on your mobile with a choice of channels of all genres – news, entertainment, sports and so on.
  • Multi-player Gaming – now you can play games and compete with other people through multi-player gaming
  • Video clips that you can download or stream smoothly while on the move

What is the difference between 3G and 4G?

These are different technologies. However, very few phones (less than 1%) are 4G compatible and hence most customers will not be able to use 4G. Also, 4G handsets are very expensive (usually more than Rs. 75,000).
3G customers will see “3”, “3G”, “H”, or “H+” next to the network bars on their phones.
4G customers should see “LTE” or “4G” next to the network bars on their phones. If any 4G customer does not see “LTE” or “4G” on their phone, then their operator is actually providing 2G or 3G service, and not 4G.

I have seen that the auction for 3G in Pakistan had different categories namely “10 Mhz” and “5 Mhz” what exactly do they mean?

These are frequencies through which user capacity is allocated on the cellular network. Jazz, being the largest network cellular operator in Pakistan, has secured twice the band (10 MHz) as compared to two other operators in the 3G auction and hence will be able to cater for more users and provide a better experience on its 3G network. 10 MHz means faster speed and more customers served in a better way.

What is the 3G license and has Jazz been given this?

Jazz has successfully won the 3G license, and it can now launch 3G services. With 3G, Jazz will offer an even better mobile internet experience for its customers.

What can I do with 3G?

3G services enable customers to use very fast internet on their phones. The same video that took about one minute to download previously, with 3G it will take less than 6 seconds. You will be able to watch videos, listen to music, stream live TV and make video calls using 3G services.

What will I have to do once I am provisioned for 3G?

Once you have activated 3G services and are subscribed to a 3G bundle, you will be able to enjoy 3G speed on mobile internet. Please restart your phone once to be able to latch on to the network.

How do I know if I am in a 3G coverage area?

Dial *443# for free and check if you are currently in a 3G coverage area. Once you are provisioned for 3G and you are in a 3G coverage area, your network signal will show ‘3G’ , ‘H’ or ‘H+’ . Also, please stay tuned to our website www.jazz.com.pk for updates on coverage areas.

What is 3G?

What is the difference between 3G and 4G?

These are different technologies. However, very few phones (less than 1%) are 4G compatible and hence most customers will not be able to use 4G. Also, 4G handsets are very expensive (usually more than Rs. 75,000).

How can I subscribe to 3G bundles?

Prepaid subscribers can view 3G bundles by dialing *443# (without any charges) & selecting option “3G Offers”. After seeing available bundles, subscriber can simply subscribe by replying with desired option.

Can 3G bundles be subscribed & consumed in both 2G & 3G networks?

How can I make Video Call using Jazz 3G network?

Video Calling is a handset based feature. Please check specifications of your handset to find if it supports Video Call. If yes, you can make Video Call while connected to the 3G network. Receiving party should be connected to 3G network and have Video Calling capable handset in order to receive Video Call.

Can I make Video call if I am not connected to 3G network?

Video Call cannot be made outside 3G network. You must be connected to 3G network to avail this service.

Can I make Video call to other operators?

Currently, Video call is available from Jazz to Jazz only.

How much will I be charged for making Video call?

Outgoing video calls will be charged at base rate of Rs. 5.98/min. Charging pulse will be 30 seconds. No charges will be applied for receiving video calls.
